Understanding Ankle Sprains

An ankle sprain occurs when the ligaments that support the ankle stretch or tear due to sudden twisting or rolling of the foot. Common causes include:

  • Sports injuries
  • Walking or running on uneven surfaces
  • Landing awkwardly after jumping
  • Sudden changes in direction

Our Treatment Approach

Phase 1: Acute Care

  • Initial assessment of injury severity
  • RICE protocol (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation)
  • Pain management techniques
  • Gentle mobilization as appropriate

Phase 2: Rehabilitation

  • Progressive strengthening exercises
  • Range of motion restoration
  • Balance and proprioception training
  • Soft tissue manipulation

Phase 3: Return to Activity

  • Sport-specific conditioning
  • Preventive exercises and techniques
  • Gait analysis and correction
  • Long-term prevention strategies

Recovery Timeline

Days 1-3

Protection, rest, and inflammation control

Days 4-14

Gentle movement and basic exercises

Weeks 2-6

Progressive strengthening and stability training

Weeks 6+

Return to normal activities with preventive care

Common Questions About Ankle Sprains

How long does recovery take?

Recovery time varies depending on the severity of the sprain, typically ranging from 2-8 weeks with proper treatment.

When should I seek treatment?

Seek immediate treatment if you experience severe pain, inability to bear weight, or significant swelling.

Will I need imaging tests?

We'll assess if X-rays or other imaging is needed based on the injury's severity and symptoms.
