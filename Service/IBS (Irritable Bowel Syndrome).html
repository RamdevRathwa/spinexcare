Understanding Irritable Bowel Syndrome

IBS is a common digestive disorder affecting millions worldwide

What is IBS?

Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) is a functional gastrointestinal disorder characterized by abdominal pain, bloating, and changes in bowel habits. It's often caused by:

  • Nervous system dysfunction affecting gut-brain communication
  • Muscle contractions in the intestine that may be too strong or weak
  • Spinal misalignments affecting nerve signals to digestive organs
  • Stress and anxiety that can trigger or worsen symptoms
  • Food sensitivities and dietary triggers

Chiropractic care can help address the underlying nerve dysfunction that contributes to IBS symptoms, providing natural relief without medication.

Recognizing IBS Symptoms

Early identification helps in better management

Abdominal Pain & Cramping

Recurrent discomfort or pain in the abdomen, often relieved by bowel movements. May range from mild to severe.

Bloating & Gas

Feeling of fullness, swelling in the abdomen, and excessive gas that can cause discomfort and social embarrassment.

Bowel Habit Changes

Alternating between diarrhea and constipation, or predominantly one pattern (IBS-D, IBS-C, or IBS-M).

Stress-Related Flare-ups

Symptoms often worsen during periods of stress, anxiety, or emotional distress due to the gut-brain connection.

Managing & Preventing IBS Flare-ups

Lifestyle modifications for better digestive health

Dietary Management

Identify and avoid trigger foods, eat smaller meals more frequently, and stay hydrated to support digestive function.

Stress Reduction

Practice relaxation techniques like meditation, deep breathing, or yoga to calm the nervous system and reduce IBS symptoms.

Regular Exercise

Engage in moderate physical activity to stimulate normal contractions of your intestines and reduce stress.

Quality Sleep

Prioritize restful sleep to support overall health and reduce stress that can trigger IBS symptoms.

Food Journaling

Keep track of foods, symptoms, and stress levels to identify patterns and personalize your management approach.
