Understanding Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

Carpal tunnel syndrome occurs when the median nerve becomes compressed as it travels through the wrist at the carpal tunnel

What is Carpal Tunnel Syndrome?

Carpal tunnel syndrome is a common condition that causes pain, numbness, and tingling in the hand and arm. It occurs when the median nerve, which runs from your forearm into your palm, becomes pressed or squeezed at the wrist.

  • Median Nerve Compression - The median nerve controls sensation to the palm side of thumb and fingers (except little finger)
  • Anatomical Factors - Smaller carpal tunnel anatomy, wrist fractures, or arthritis that reduce space for the nerve
  • Repetitive Hand Use - Prolonged repetitive wrist motions from typing, assembly work, or using vibrating tools
  • Health Conditions - Diabetes, rheumatoid arthritis, thyroid disorders, and fluid retention during pregnancy
  • Workplace Factors - Poor ergonomics, prolonged computer use, and awkward wrist positions
  • Nerve-Damaging Conditions - Alcoholism, vitamin B6 deficiency, and other conditions that damage nerves

Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial to prevent permanent nerve damage and maintain hand function.

Recognizing Carpal Tunnel Symptoms

Carpal tunnel syndrome typically develops gradually with distinctive symptoms

Numbness & Tingling

Pins and needles sensation in thumb, index, middle, and ring fingers, often worse at night or upon waking.

Electric Shock Sensations

Shooting pains that radiate from wrist up the arm or down into the fingers, often triggered by specific movements.

Weakness & Clumsiness

Difficulty gripping objects, frequent dropping of items, and reduced hand strength, especially in the thumb.

Nighttime Symptoms

Pain and numbness that wakes you from sleep, often relieved by shaking or moving the hands.

Preventing Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

Proactive strategies to protect your wrists and maintain nerve health

Proper Typing Technique

Keep wrists straight, elbows at 90 degrees, and use light touch when typing. Take frequent breaks to stretch.

Neutral Wrist Position

Maintain straight wrist alignment during activities. Avoid bending wrists up, down, or to the sides for prolonged periods.

Frequent Rest Breaks

Take 2-3 minute breaks every 20-30 minutes during repetitive tasks. Perform wrist stretches during breaks.

Keep Hands Warm

Cold temperatures can worsen symptoms. Wear fingerless gloves in cold environments to maintain circulation.

Maintain Good Posture

Proper shoulder and neck alignment reduces nerve tension that can contribute to carpal tunnel symptoms.

Nighttime Wrist Support

Use wrist splints at night to maintain neutral position and prevent bending that compresses the nerve during sleep.
