Understanding Sciatica

Sciatica refers to pain that radiates along the path of the sciatic nerve, which branches from your lower back through your hips and buttocks and down each leg

What is Sciatica?

Sciatica isn't a medical condition itself, but rather a symptom of an underlying problem involving compression or irritation of the sciatic nerve - the longest and thickest nerve in your body. This compression causes inflammation, pain, and often some numbness in the affected leg.

  • Lumbar Herniated Disc - The most common cause where a disc bulges and presses on the sciatic nerve root
  • Piriformis Syndrome - When the piriformis muscle in the buttocks spasms and compresses the sciatic nerve
  • Spinal Stenosis - Narrowing of the spinal canal that puts pressure on the nerves
  • Spondylolisthesis - When one vertebra slips forward over another, pinching the nerve
  • Degenerative Disc Disease - Age-related breakdown of discs that can lead to nerve compression

Proper diagnosis is crucial since treatment varies depending on the underlying cause of your sciatic nerve compression.

Recognizing Sciatica Symptoms

Sciatica symptoms can vary from mild annoyance to severe, debilitating pain

Shooting Leg Pain

Electric shock-like pain that radiates from lower back through buttock and down the back of one leg, often following nerve pathway.

Burning & Tingling

Burning sensation, pins and needles, or numbness anywhere along the sciatic nerve pathway from lower back to foot.

Leg Weakness

Difficulty moving the affected leg or foot, feeling of heaviness, or trouble standing up from seated position.

Worsening with Sitting

Pain typically intensifies when sitting for prolonged periods, while standing or walking may provide some relief.

Preventing Sciatica Recurrence

Lifestyle modifications to protect your sciatic nerve long-term

Proper Sitting Posture

Use lumbar support, keep feet flat, and take standing breaks every 30 minutes to reduce pressure on sciatic nerve.

Core Strengthening

Regular exercises for abdominal and back muscles to support spine and prevent disc issues that cause sciatica.

Safe Lifting Technique

Bend knees, keep back straight, and hold objects close to body. Avoid twisting while lifting heavy items.

Sleep Positioning

Sleep on side with pillow between knees or on back with pillow under knees to maintain spinal alignment.

Regular Movement

Avoid prolonged sitting. Walk regularly to keep spine flexible and improve circulation to nerves.

Weight Management

Maintain healthy weight to reduce stress on spine and decrease risk of disc problems that cause sciatica.
