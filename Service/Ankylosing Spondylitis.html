Understanding Ankylosing Spondylitis

A chronic inflammatory condition that primarily affects the spine and sacroiliac joints

What is Ankylosing Spondylitis?

Ankylosing Spondylitis (AS) is a type of inflammatory arthritis that primarily affects the spine, causing chronic inflammation, pain, and stiffness. Over time, this inflammation can lead to new bone formation and eventual fusion of vertebrae. Key characteristics include:

  • Chronic inflammation of spinal joints and sacroiliac joints
  • Progressive stiffness and reduced spinal mobility
  • Potential fusion of vertebrae (ankylosis) in advanced stages
  • Systemic involvement including eyes, heart, and lungs in some cases
  • Genetic predisposition with strong association to HLA-B27 gene
  • Onset typically in young adulthood (late teens to early 30s)

Chiropractic care focuses on maintaining spinal mobility, reducing inflammation, managing pain, and preventing postural complications through specialized techniques and rehabilitation.

Recognizing Ankylosing Spondylitis Symptoms

Early recognition and intervention are crucial for managing this progressive condition

Morning Stiffness

Significant stiffness and pain upon waking that improves with movement and activity throughout the day.

Chronic Back Pain

Persistent dull pain in the lower back and buttocks, often worse during periods of inactivity and at night.

Reduced Spinal Mobility

Gradual loss of flexibility in the spine, difficulty bending, twisting, or extending the back fully.

Fatigue & Systemic Symptoms

Persistent tiredness, and in some cases, involvement of other joints, eyes (uveitis), or digestive system.

Understanding AS Progression

Early intervention can significantly impact long-term outcomes

Early Stage

Inflammatory Phase

Intermittent pain and stiffness, primarily in sacroiliac joints and lower back. Symptoms improve with activity.

  • Best opportunity for mobility preservation
  • Focus on maintaining full range of motion
  • Excellent response to conservative care
Moderate Stage

Progressive Stiffness

More consistent pain and stiffness, beginning to affect daily activities. Early postural changes may appear.

  • Focus on preventing fusion
  • Maintaining functional mobility
  • Managing pain and inflammation
Advanced Stage

Structural Changes

Significant spinal stiffness, possible fusion, and postural changes (kyphosis). Multiple joint involvement common.

  • Focus on pain management
  • Preserving remaining mobility
  • Improving quality of life

Daily Management Strategies

Essential practices for living well with ankylosing spondylitis

Morning Routine

Focus: Managing morning stiffness

  • Warm shower or bath to reduce stiffness
  • Gentle stretching before getting out of bed
  • Slow, gradual movement to start the day
  • Maintain consistent wake-up time

Daily Activities

Focus: Maintaining mobility throughout the day

  • Frequent position changes when sitting
  • Regular stretching breaks during work
  • Proper posture and ergonomic setup
  • Pacing activities to avoid overexertion

Evening & Sleep

Focus: Preparing for restful sleep

  • Sleep on firm mattress with proper support
  • Gentle evening stretching routine
  • Avoid sleeping in curled positions
  • Use pillows strategically for support

Living Well with Ankylosing Spondylitis

Comprehensive strategies for managing AS and maintaining quality of life

Regular Exercise

Maintain consistent physical activity including swimming, walking, and specific AS exercises to preserve mobility and reduce stiffness.

Posture Awareness

Conscious effort to maintain upright posture, avoid prolonged flexion, and use ergonomic supports during work and rest.

Anti-Inflammatory Diet

Focus on whole foods, omega-3 fatty acids, and anti-inflammatory nutrients while reducing processed foods and sugar.

Smoking Cessation

Avoid smoking completely, as it significantly worsens AS progression and reduces treatment effectiveness.

Sleep Management

Sleep on back with minimal pillows to maintain spinal alignment and prevent flexion deformities during rest.

Consistent Care

Regular chiropractic maintenance to monitor progression, adjust treatment, and address new symptoms promptly.
