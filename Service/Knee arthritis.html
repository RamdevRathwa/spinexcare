Understanding Knee Arthritis & Pain

Knee pain affects millions and can significantly impact your quality of life and mobility

What Causes Knee Pain?

Knee pain can result from various conditions, with osteoarthritis being the most common. The knee joint is complex and vulnerable to injury and degeneration due to its weight-bearing function and range of motion.

  • Osteoarthritis - Wear-and-tear arthritis causing cartilage breakdown and bone-on-bone friction
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis - Autoimmune condition causing joint inflammation and cartilage damage
  • Post-Traumatic Arthritis - Develops after knee injuries like fractures, ligament tears, or meniscus damage
  • Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome - Pain around the kneecap from muscle imbalance or overuse
  • Meniscus Tears - Damage to the cartilage cushions that absorb shock in the knee joint
  • Ligament Injuries - Sprains or tears in ACL, PCL, MCL, or LCL ligaments

Early intervention with proper chiropractic care can significantly slow progression and improve joint function.

Recognizing Knee Arthritis Symptoms

Early identification helps in better management and prevention of further joint damage

Pain & Tenderness

Deep, aching pain that worsens with activity, tenderness when pressing on the joint, and discomfort that may radiate.

Stiffness & Reduced Mobility

Morning stiffness lasting more than 30 minutes, difficulty bending or straightening the knee fully, and limited range of motion.

Swelling & Inflammation

Visible swelling around the knee, warmth to touch, and fluid accumulation that makes the joint feel tight and full.

Functional Limitations

Difficulty with stairs, rising from chairs, walking long distances, and performing daily activities without pain or instability.

Managing & Preventing Knee Arthritis Progression

Proactive strategies to protect your knee joints and maintain mobility

Weight Management

Every extra pound puts 4 pounds of pressure on knees. Maintain healthy weight to significantly reduce joint stress.

Low-Impact Exercise

Choose swimming, cycling, or elliptical training instead of high-impact activities like running on hard surfaces.

Strength Training

Strengthen quadriceps, hamstrings, and hip muscles to provide better support and stability for knee joints.

Proper Footwear

Wear supportive shoes with good cushioning to absorb shock and maintain proper leg alignment during walking.

Anti-Inflammatory Diet

Include omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants, and anti-inflammatory foods to reduce joint inflammation naturally.

Regular Stretching

Maintain flexibility in hamstrings, calves, and quadriceps to prevent muscle imbalances that stress knee joints.
