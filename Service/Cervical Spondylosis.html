Understanding Cervical Spondylosis

Cervical spondylosis is a common age-related condition affecting the cervical spine (neck), causing degeneration of discs and joints.

What Causes Cervical Spondylosis?

This condition typically develops as part of the natural aging process, but can be accelerated by various factors:

  • Age-related disc degeneration
  • Poor posture and ergonomics
  • Previous neck injuries
  • Occupational strain
  • Genetic factors

Common Symptoms

Recognize the signs of cervical spondylosis for early intervention and better outcomes

Neck Pain and Stiffness

Chronic neck pain that may worsen with movement and prolonged positions

Radiating Symptoms

Pain, tingling, or numbness radiating to shoulders, arms, and hands

Headaches and Dizziness

Frequent headaches, particularly at the base of the skull, and occasional dizziness
