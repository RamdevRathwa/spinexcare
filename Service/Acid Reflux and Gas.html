Understanding Acid Reflux & Digestive Gas

The surprising connection between spinal health and digestive function

What Causes Acid Reflux & Gas?

Acid reflux (GERD) and excessive gas are common digestive issues that often have underlying neurological causes. The vagus nerve, which controls digestive function, can be compromised by spinal misalignments, leading to improper stomach valve function and digestive enzyme production.

Common Digestive Issues We Address:

  • Acid Reflux (GERD) - Stomach acid flowing back into esophagus
  • Heartburn - Burning sensation in chest after eating
  • Bloating & Gas - Excessive gas production and abdominal distension
  • Indigestion - Discomfort and feeling of fullness after meals
  • Burping & Belching - Frequent air release from stomach

The Spinal Connection to Digestion:

  • Vagus Nerve Interference - Spinal misalignments can compress the vagus nerve, disrupting digestive signals
  • Diaphragm Function - Thoracic spine issues can affect diaphragm movement and lower esophageal sphincter function
  • Stress Response - Spinal tension can activate sympathetic nervous system, slowing digestion
  • Organ Function - Nerve interference can reduce stomach and intestinal efficiency

Chiropractic care addresses the root neurological causes of digestive issues, providing lasting relief without medication side effects.

Recognizing Acid Reflux & Gas Symptoms

Common signs that indicate underlying digestive nervous system issues

Burning Sensation

Heartburn feeling in chest, often after meals or when lying down. Acid taste in mouth and frequent burping with acid content.

Abdominal Bloating

Feeling of fullness and tightness in abdomen, visible distension, and discomfort that worsens throughout the day.

Excessive Gas

Frequent belching, flatulence, and gurgling sounds in abdomen. Often accompanied by abdominal cramping and relief after gas release.

Nighttime Symptoms

Worsening of reflux when lying down, nighttime coughing, disrupted sleep, and morning hoarseness or sore throat.

The Nerve-Digestion Connection

How spinal health directly impacts your digestive system

Vagus Nerve (Cranial Nerve X)

Function: Controls stomach acid production, digestive enzyme release, and intestinal motility

Spinal Connection: Originates from brainstem, affected by upper cervical misalignments

When Compromised: Slowed digestion, reduced enzyme production, improper stomach emptying

Thoracic Nerves (T5-T9)

Function: Control esophageal function, stomach valve operation, and stomach motility

Spinal Connection: Mid-back misalignments affect nerve signals to upper digestive organs

When Compromised: Acid reflux, heartburn, poor stomach emptying

Lumbar Nerves (L1-L3)

Function: Regulate small intestine and colon function, gas movement, and bloating control

Spinal Connection: Lower back issues affect lower digestive tract coordination

When Compromised: Gas buildup, bloating, irregular bowel movements

Proper nerve function is essential for optimal digestion - when spinal misalignments interfere, digestive issues often result

Home Management & Prevention Strategies

Daily practices to support digestive health and prevent recurrence

Mindful Eating Habits

Eat slowly, chew thoroughly, avoid lying down immediately after meals, and maintain upright posture during and after eating.

Digestive-Friendly Nutrition

Include fiber-rich foods, fermented foods, and avoid trigger foods like spicy, fatty, or acidic items that may worsen reflux.

Proper Hydration

Drink adequate water between meals (not during), avoid carbonated beverages, and limit caffeine and alcohol intake.

Sleep Positioning

Elevate head of bed 6-8 inches, sleep on left side to reduce reflux, and avoid eating within 3 hours of bedtime.

Regular Movement

Gentle walking after meals, regular exercise to reduce stress, and specific exercises to improve diaphragm function and core stability.

Stress Management

Practice relaxation techniques, deep breathing exercises, and maintain consistent chiropractic care to support nervous system balance.
