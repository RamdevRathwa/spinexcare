Understanding Disc Bulge Conditions

A disc bulge occurs when spinal discs become compressed and extend beyond their normal space, potentially causing pain and nerve compression.

About Cervical & Lumbar Disc Bulge

Disc bulges can occur in both the neck (cervical) and lower back (lumbar) regions, affecting daily activities and quality of life. Understanding the condition is key to effective treatment.

  • Cervical disc bulges affect the neck region, causing neck pain and arm symptoms
  • Lumbar disc bulges occur in the lower back, potentially causing back pain and leg symptoms
  • Both conditions can result from age, injury, or repetitive strain
  • Early intervention can prevent progression and chronic pain

Common Symptoms

Recognize the signs of cervical and lumbar disc bulge conditions

Cervical Symptoms

  • Neck pain and stiffness
  • Arm pain or tingling
  • Headaches
  • Shoulder blade pain

Lumbar Symptoms

  • Lower back pain
  • Leg pain or sciatica
  • Numbness or tingling
  • Muscle weakness

General Signs

  • Pain worsening with certain movements
  • Morning stiffness
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Activity limitations
