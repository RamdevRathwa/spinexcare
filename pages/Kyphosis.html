Understanding Kyphosis

Kyphosis is an excessive outward curvature of the spine that causes rounding of the upper back. While some curvature is normal, excessive kyphosis can lead to problems.

Types and Causes

  • Postural kyphosis from poor posture habits
  • Structural kyphosis from vertebral changes
  • Age-related kyphosis (senile kyphosis)
  • Scheuermann's disease in adolescents
  • Congenital kyphosis present at birth

Physical Changes

  • Rounded upper back appearance
  • Forward head posture
  • Uneven shoulder heights
  • Visible spine curvature

Pain and Discomfort

  • Upper back pain
  • Neck strain
  • Muscle fatigue
  • Stiffness

Associated Issues

  • Breathing difficulties
  • Reduced flexibility
  • Digestive problems
  • Self-image concerns
