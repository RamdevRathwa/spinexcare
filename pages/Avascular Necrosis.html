Understanding Avascular Necrosis (AVN)

Avascular Necrosis, also known as osteonecrosis, occurs when blood supply to the bone is interrupted, leading to bone tissue death. Common symptoms include:

  • Progressive joint pain, especially in hips and knees
  • Limited range of motion
  • Difficulty bearing weight
  • Joint stiffness and discomfort
  • Pain that worsens with activity

Common Questions About AVN Treatment

What causes Avascular Necrosis?

AVN can be caused by injury, long-term medication use, excessive alcohol consumption, or certain medical conditions that affect blood flow to the bones.

Can chiropractic care help with AVN?

Yes, chiropractic care can help manage pain, maintain joint mobility, and slow progression when combined with proper medical management.

How long does treatment take?

Treatment duration varies based on severity and stage of AVN. We'll create a personalized treatment plan after thorough evaluation.
