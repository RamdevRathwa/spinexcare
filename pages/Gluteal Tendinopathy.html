Understanding Gluteal Tendinopathy

Gluteal Tendinopathy is a common condition causing pain on the outer hip due to irritation or degeneration of the gluteal tendons.

About the Condition

This condition affects the tendons that connect the gluteal muscles to the hip bone, often causing significant discomfort and limiting daily activities.

  • Commonly affects adults aged 40-60
  • More prevalent in women and athletes
  • Can be aggravated by prolonged sitting or standing
  • Often associated with repetitive activities
  • May develop gradually over time
