Understanding Scoliosis

Scoliosis is a three-dimensional curvature of the spine that can affect people of all ages, particularly developing children and adolescents.

About Scoliosis

Scoliosis can develop for various reasons and may progress over time if left untreated. Early detection and appropriate intervention are crucial for optimal outcomes.

  • Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (most common)
  • Congenital Scoliosis (present at birth)
  • Neuromuscular Scoliosis
  • Adult Degenerative Scoliosis
  • Functional Scoliosis

Common Signs & Symptoms

Recognizing the signs of scoliosis early can lead to better treatment outcomes.

Visual Signs

  • Uneven shoulders
  • One hip higher than the other
  • Asymmetrical waistline
  • Protruding shoulder blade

Physical Symptoms

  • Back pain
  • Muscle fatigue
  • Postural changes
  • Breathing difficulties

Related Issues

  • Reduced flexibility
  • Activity limitations
  • Body image concerns
  • Quality of life impact
