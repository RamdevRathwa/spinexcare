Understanding Cervicogenic Headaches

Cervicogenic headaches originate from neck problems but manifest as head pain, often mimicking other types of headaches.

What Causes Cervicogenic Headaches?

These headaches stem from disorders or lesions in the cervical spine and its component bone, disc, and soft tissue elements. Common causes include:

  • Neck injury or trauma
  • Poor posture and ergonomics
  • Cervical spine disorders
  • Muscle tension and stress
  • Occupational factors

Common Symptoms

Recognize the distinctive signs of cervicogenic headaches

Head & Neck Pain

One-sided head pain that often starts in the neck and radiates to the head and face

Movement Sensitivity

Pain that worsens with certain neck movements or positions

Associated Symptoms

Neck stiffness, shoulder pain, and reduced range of motion
