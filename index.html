Problems We Treat

Back Pain

Lower back pain is very common.Lower back pain affects the lumbar region of your spine or back. It can result from many different injuries and conditions. Most often, the cause is an injury to muscles or tendons in your back.

NeckPain

Neck pain refers to discomfort or soreness in the cervical (neck) region of the spine, which can range from mild to severe and affect various structures like muscles, nerves, bones, and joints. It can be caused by a wide range of factors, including injuries, poor posture, muscle strains, nerve compression, and underlying medical conditions.

Cervical/Lumbar Disc Bulge/disc Herniation

A disc bulge occurs when the soft inner part of a spinal disc pushes out through a weak spot in the outer layer. This can irritate nearby nerves, causing back pain, sciatica, or numbness in arms or legs. Chiropractic treatment helps relieve pressure, reduce pain, and restore proper spinal function.

Cervical Spondylosis

Cervical spondylosis, also known as neck arthritis, is a common, age-related condition characterized by the degeneration of discs and joints in the neck (cervical spine).

Sciatica

Sciatica pain refers to pain that radiates along the sciatic nerve, which runs from the lower back down the back of each leg

Heel Pain

Heel pain is a common ailment with various potential causes, the most frequent being plantar fasciitis, an inflammation of the tissue connecting the heel to the toes

Knee Arthritis/Pain

Knee arthritis refers to inflammation and deterioration of the knee joint, primarily the cartilage

Frozen Shoulder

Frozen shoulder, also known as adhesive capsulitis, is a condition characterized by pain and stiffness in the shoulder joint, limiting its range of motion

Posture Correction

Improve your body alignment and reduce strain by correcting poor posture habits with personalized therapy.

Cervicogenic Headache

Cervicogenic headache is a secondary headache caused by neck disorders, often radiating pain from the neck to the back of the head and around the eyes, worsened by neck movement.

Rotator Cuff Injury/Shoulder Pain

A rotator cuff injury involves damage to the muscles and tendons that stabilize the shoulder. It can cause pain, weakness, and limited shoulder movement, especially during overhead activities.

Trapezitis

Trapezitis is the inflammation of the trapezius muscle, often caused by poor posture, stress, or repetitive strain. It leads to neck, shoulder, and upper back pain, especially after prolonged sitting or screen use.

TMJ / Jaw Pain

Effective treatment for jaw tension, TMJ disorders, and facial pain to restore smooth jaw function.

Sports Injury

Recovery programs for ligament tears, muscle strains, and sports-related injuries to help you return stronger.

Body Stiffness Relief

Loosen up tight muscles and improve joint flexibility to move freely and comfortably again.

Migraine

Reduce frequency and intensity of migraines and chronic headaches with safe, non-invasive therapies.

Tennis Elbow

Treat pain and inflammation from repetitive elbow use with targeted exercises and soft tissue work.

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

Alleviate wrist pain, tingling, and numbness with gentle mobilization and ergonomic corrections.

Plantar Fasciitis

Specialized therapy for heel pain and inflammation of the plantar fascia to restore foot comfort and mobility.

SI-HIP Joint Pain

Effective treatment for sacroiliac and hip joint dysfunction, relieving pain and improving pelvic alignment.

Body Alignment

Correcting postural imbalances by aligning the spine, shoulders, hips, and knees for optimal body mechanics and movement.

Spondylosis

Non-surgical treatment for age-related spinal wear and tear, reducing stiffness and chronic neck or back pain.

Spondylitis

Targeted therapy for spinal inflammation, improving flexibility and reducing discomfort caused by spondylitis.

Lordosis

Correction and management of excessive inward spinal curvature (lordosis) to improve posture and core stability.

Spondylolisthesis

Therapy and stabilization for vertebral slippage, helping to reduce back pain and improve spinal alignment.

Kyphosis

Treatment for rounded upper back (kyphosis), focused on posture correction and spinal strengthening.

Scoliosis

Non-surgical scoliosis care, including exercise-based correction and posture training for spinal balance.

Acid Reflux and Gas

Acid reflux and gas are digestive issues often caused by poor diet, stress, or gut imbalances. Symptoms include heartburn, bloating, and discomfort in the upper abdomen.

IBS (Irritable Bowel Syndrome)

IBS is a chronic digestive disorder that causes abdominal pain, bloating, and changes in bowel habits like diarrhea or constipation, often triggered by food, stress, or hormones.

Ankle Sprain

Ankle sprain occurs when the ligaments supporting the ankle stretch or tear, typically due to a sudden twist or fall. Symptoms include swelling, bruising, and pain with movement.

Gluteal Tendinopathy

Gluteal tendinopathy is a condition affecting the tendons of the gluteus medius and minimus muscles, leading to pain on the outer hip, especially during walking, climbing stairs, or lying on the side.

IT Band Syndrome

IT Band Syndrome is an overuse injury of the connective tissues located on the outer thigh and knee, causing pain, inflammation, and tightness especially during running or cycling.

Reduced Stress

Stress can affect both your mind and body. Chiropractic care lowers cortisol levels, releases tension from the spine, and restores nervous system balance—helping you feel calmer and more in control.

Better Sleep

Chiropractic adjustments help improve sleep quality by reducing tension, promoting spinal alignment, and calming the nervous system—helping you fall asleep faster and sleep deeper.

Avascular Necrosis

Avascular necrosis (AVN), also called osteonecrosis, is a condition where bone tissue dies due to lack of blood supply. It commonly affects the hip joint and can lead to joint pain and collapse if untreated.

Ankylosing Spondylitis

Ankylosing spondylitis is a chronic inflammatory arthritis that primarily affects the spine and sacroiliac joints. It can cause back pain, stiffness, and in severe cases, fusion of the spinal vertebrae.

Therapies & Techniques

Chiropractor

Chiropractic is a natural, hands-on healthcare approach focused on diagnosing, treating, and preventing mechanical disorders of the musculoskeletal system—especially the spine. Chiropractors use manual adjustments and other techniques to align the spine, reduce pain, improve mobility, and support the body’s natural healing abilities without the use of drugs or surgery

Musculoskeletal Assestment and Treatment

Musculoskeletal assessment involves evaluating the body's muscles, bones, joints, and related soft tissues to identify impairments and functional limitations

Customized Treatment Plans

We offer personalized chiropractic treatment plans tailored to your unique pain points, posture, and lifestyle. Whether it’s injury recovery, chronic pain, or posture correction, our goal is lasting relief through care that evolves with your progress.

Physiotherapy

a healthcare profession focused on restoring, maintaining, or increasing physical mobility, strength, and function

Pain Management

Comprehensive approach to relieve chronic pain in the back, neck, joints, and muscles using modern techniques.

Tapping

Taping provides support to muscles and joints without restricting movement.

Cupping Therapy

Cupping uses suction to improve blood flow, release muscle tightness, and reduce inflammation.

Needling Therapy

Dry needling involves inserting thin, sterile needles into trigger points (tight knots in muscles) to release tension, reduce pain, and improve movement.

IASTM(MFR)

a therapeutic technique that uses specialized tools to manipulate muscles, tendons, and ligaments
